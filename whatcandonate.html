Organs That Can Be Donated

Heart

The heart is one of the most vital organs that can be donated. It can be transplanted to save a person's life.

Heart transplantation is a complex procedure that requires a compatible donor and recipient.

Patients with heart failure often benefit from heart transplants to improve their quality of life.

Blood

Blood donation involves giving a portion of your blood to be used for various medical purposes.

It's essential for surgeries, emergencies, and treating various medical conditions.

Blood donors are screened for eligibility, and the process is safe and straightforward.

Kidney

Of all organs in the human body, the demand for kidneys is the highest, and kidneys are the most frequently donated organs.

Kidneys are responsible for filtering waste and excess water from the blood and balancing the bodyâ€™s fluids.

Those on dialysis due to suffering from high blood pressure, diabetes or cystic kidney disease may be candidates for a kidney transplant.

Liver

Because of the vast number of functions, donating a liver can save people from chronic liver infections.

The liver has more than 500 known functions in the human body

A donated liver can sometimes be split between two recipients, which means one donor could potentially save the lives of two people.

Lungs

When you breathe in your lungs fill with air, and oxygen from that air moves into your blood, allowing it to be carried to all parts of the body.

When you breathe out, a waste gas called carbon dioxide is removed by the lungs. This exchange of gases is vital for survival.

If you choose to donate your lungs when you die you could save someone's life.

Pancreas

At present, the majority of pancreas transplants are performed on people who have Type 1 Diabetes which can also cause kidney failure.

The pancreas is an organ of the digestive system and endocrine system of vertebrates.

The pancreas is a mixed or heterocrine gland, i.e., it has both an endocrine and a digestive exocrine function.
